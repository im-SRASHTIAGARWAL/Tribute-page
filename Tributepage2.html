About Dr. Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Widely loved as the "People's President", he inspired millions with his humility, vision, and dedication to the nation.

His Contributions

  • Played a pivotal role in India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.
  • Known as the “Missile Man of India” for his work on Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Promoted education, innovation, and youth empowerment throughout his life.
  • Served as President of India (2002–2007), remembered for his simplicity and accessibility.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam believed in the power of dreams and youth. His books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds continue to inspire generations. He passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture to students, symbolizing his lifelong commitment to education.
